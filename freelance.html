Choosing Between Web Developer and Web Designer

This has always been a topic of hot debate amound techs, making it a lil harder for newbies to decide early on in their coding learning journey what they would want to be. This prompted me to do some researches on this subject, and this article sumarise my thoughts.

A Designer's Job

A web designer uses graphics and graphic design software to create a look for the web. This design is then married with coding to bring it to life online.The designer may not always be the person writing the code.

Much of a designer’s job is creative and uses both intuition and imagination, often characteristics of people who are considered right-brained.
The best designers have a strong grasp on a variety of concepts including color and typography, spacial relationships, audience and user experience.

A Developer's Job

A web developer builds the backbone of websites, typically from the ground up, and knows languages specific to the web. HTML, Javascript, and CSS are among the tools in their kit.
Skills from technical ability and thinking to logic are an essential part of succesful web developers repertoires.

While degrees in a variety of fields such as computer science or programming used to be required, a high demand in developers positions lead employers to turn towards bootcamps that have the advantage of a solid hands on training over college institutions since most employers will require a portfolio during the hiring process.

Two Jobs,Same Goals

At the end of the day, both web designers and web developers are working toward a singular goal – to create a website or app that entices and attracts users.
The defined lines between designers and developers are becoming more blurred as more designers are learning to code and more developers are paying close attention to design theory.

One of the toughest parts about web design and development can be working together and communicating in a way that everyone understands.
